What is Healthcare Heroes?

One email. One message worth sharing.

Healthcare Heroes is a peer-to-peer health movement in Weymouth & Portland. Every month, a small group of local people get one simple, human message about health and wellbeing — and share it with the people they care about.

No jargon. No appointments. No pressure. Just a small kindness, passed from one person to another.

That's it. That's the whole idea.

Did you know?

People are far more likely to seek support when someone they trust mentions it — not when they see a poster or a leaflet. One conversation from someone who cares is worth more than a hundred campaigns.

This month we're looking at

Mental health and young people.

One in six young people in England will experience a mental health problem in any given week. Many of them won't say anything — not because they don't want help, but because they don't know where to start, or they're worried about what people will think.

The people best placed to help them aren't always professionals. They're often the people already in their lives — a parent, a grandparent, a neighbour, a friend. Someone who notices. Someone who says something.

That someone could be you.

Signs to watch for

Withdrawing from friends and family. Changes in sleep or appetite. Loss of interest in things they used to enjoy. Difficulty concentrating. Increased irritability or low mood. Talking about feeling hopeless. Trust your instincts — if something feels different, it probably is.

You don't need to have the answers. You just need to be willing to have the conversation. A simple "I've noticed you seem a bit down lately — are you okay?" can open a door that changes everything.
